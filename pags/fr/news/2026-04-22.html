New Global Landscape: From "Price War" to "System Competition

Fasteners have evolved from basic industrial components into strategic core parts. Competition now focuses on the comprehensive integration of product mix, manufacturing efficiency, delivery capability, and global service

Accueil / Actualités / New Global Landscape: From "Price War" to "System Competition
Publié le 22 avril 2026

For decades, "price" was the sharpest weapon for Chinese fastener companies. But that path is now reaching its end. Tariff frictions, raw material volatility, and customers' almost demanding requirements for reliability have made "cheap" no longer a ticket to success. The global fastener market is undergoing a profound power shift — from a "price war" to a "system competition." Players relying solely on cost advantages are being gradually marginalized, replaced by systemic competitors with four pillars: product mix, manufacturing efficiency, delivery capability, and global service.

1. Market data: A structural turning point in the billion-dollar赛道

According to the latest 2025 industrial statistics, the global industrial fastener market has exceeded $109.6 billion, and this figure is expanding at a steady CAGR of 6.3%. Forecasts indicate that by 2035, the total market will reach $189.9 billion. The growth is not uniform — high-end fasteners (automotive safety parts, aerospace titanium alloys, wind power high-strength bolts) account for more than 60% of profit growth, while low-end standard parts are stuck in extreme involution. This means: scale alone no longer matters; system capability determines the ranking.

  • 🚗 New energy vehicles — Fastener usage per vehicle is 20%-30% higher than in traditional fuel vehicles, with corrosion resistance and lightweighting driving unit prices up by more than 35%.
  • ✈️ Aerospace — Full lifecycle traceability is required, and the value of a single piece can be hundreds of times that of ordinary fasteners, with extremely high certification barriers.
  • ⚙️ Industrial robots & automation — Demanding preload consistency and fatigue life, precision fasteners are growing at over 12% per year.
  • 🌍 Export structure upgrade — Companies with ISO/TS 16949 and aviation certification see order premium capabilities increase by 40%-70%.

The traditional "one ton per container" rough export model is being replaced by "technical service + localized inventory + quality agreements." European and American OEMs no longer ask "what's the lowest price," but instead demand suppliers demonstrate complete FMEA, process capability indices, and simultaneous engineering capabilities across global factories.

2. What exactly does "system competition" involve? A deep dive into the four pillars

Product mix — Upgrade from "being able to make" to "making well, making complete, and making differentiated." Companies need to cover different strength grades (grade 8.8 ~ grade 12.9 and above), various surface treatments (zinc-nickel, Dacromet, anti-loosening coatings), and the ability to customize non-standard shapes. The more complete the product matrix, the stronger the ability to lock in major customers.

Manufacturing efficiency — Rely on single-machine automation, cold heading online monitoring, and MES systems to achieve full traceability. Domestic leading companies have achieved a 50%+ increase in per capita output value, with defect rates controlled within 50 PPM. This is no longer just "cost" but the foundation of delivery confidence.

Delivery capability — Amid global supply chain fluctuations, on-time delivery rate has become a core KPI. Leading companies establish VMI (vendor managed inventory) and overseas forward warehouses, compressing lead times from 45 days to 7-10 days. The cost of line stoppage due to stockouts far outweighs the product price difference.

Global service — On-site technical engineer support, multilingual after-sales teams, and local testing assistance. Customers are buying a complete "connection safety" solution, not just screws.

3. Winner profile and strategies for SMEs under the new landscape

  • 📌 Winner profile — Product coverage across three golden tracks: automotive, new energy, and construction machinery; CNAS-accredited lab and simulation assembly capability; overseas subsidiaries or joint ventures with localized operations experience; R&D investment ratio >4%.
  • 📌 SME strategies — Abandon the "big and all-encompassing" illusion, focus on niche scenarios (e.g., PV mounting fasteners, energy storage battery connectors); join Tier 2/3 supporting systems of leading companies; use digital tools to improve internal flow efficiency and gradually achieve data transparency.
  • 📌 New supply chain collaboration models — More OEMs are adopting "system audits + long-term framework agreements," eliminating pure price bidding and moving toward joint cost reduction and technology sharing partnerships.
  • 📌 Geopolitical risk hedging — Assembly in Southeast Asia or Mexico + localized heat treatment, replacing single export dependence with regional manufacturing to maintain an agile supply chain.

4. The next three years: How to implement the system competition?

Digital tools — Low-code platforms enable end-to-end visibility from quotation-order-production-shipment, allowing customers to track batch status in real time, greatly enhancing trust.

Certification barriers brought forward — Without IATF 16949 or AS9100D, it's nearly impossible to enter mainstream supplier lists. System certifications have shifted from "nice-to-have" to "entry tickets."

Upgraded talent structure — Need teams with a combination of materials engineering, international trade, and digital thinking. Traditional salespeople are being replaced by "technical account managers."

Green manufacturing system — Carbon footprint calculation and recycled material usage ratio are gradually becoming mandatory bidding indicators. System-oriented companies are proactively laying out circular packaging and low-carbon processes.

When price advantage is no longer rare, system capability becomes the real moat. The global fastener market is rewarding long-termists — those who don't chase the lowest quote but instead refine product, efficiency, delivery, and service into an organic whole. In the next decade, only companies that complete this "system evolution" will truly master pricing power in the new landscape.
